my-AI-stro

A local-first, self-improving personal knowledge system that turns school lessons into a queryable Source of Truth — engineered as notes for an open-book final review where LLMs are explicitly disallowed.

The maestro — it conducts your knowledge.

The story

Notes you can carry into a no-LLM exam.

Final reviews in my program are open-book — you can bring your notes — but using an LLM during the review is cheating. The act of writing notes by hand is the studying (the generation effect). So I built a system that captures each lesson into a structured Source of Truth weeks before the review, surrounds the LLM with deterministic gates to keep the notes honest, and lets me chat with my own notes. The LLM does the capture early; the cold artifact I carry in is the notes themselves.

It's also the thing I'm being trained to build. I'm in school for AI software engineering, so I engineered AI software — the software produces my notes, and the notes are the allowed review aid. The first of the MoreSalamander family, and the one the others are siblings of.

How it works

Ingest, ground, query — with judge-separated models.
lesson  →  capture + deterministic gates  →  Source of Truth  →  quiz · advisor · graph · chat · Obsidian export

The capture pipeline uses three local models with judge-separation — the model that proposes a note is never the one that checks it — so the SOT stays grounded instead of drifting into whatever the model felt like saying. Everything is local; the notes are yours and stay on your machine.

Source of Truth

Each lesson captured into a structured, queryable record — the spine everything else reads from.

Quiz & advisor

Self-test against your own notes, and an advisor that points you at what's weak — grounded in the SOT.

Knowledge graph

The connections between lessons, visualized — see how concepts hang together.

Obsidian export

Your notes leave as portable Markdown, so the artifact outlives the app.

The idea underneath

The LLM proposes; deterministic gates keep it honest.

my-AI-stro is the first instance of the MoreSalamander Deterministic Scaffold thesis: an LLM is a powerful but unreliable proposer, so you wrap it in checks that decide what's allowed to become truth. Here the stakes are personal — these notes are what you study from, and what you're allowed to bring to the exam, so they had better be right.
